Why We Built This Tracker

Amazon product listings are notoriously bad at disclosing what matters most for storage buyers. CMR vs SMR? Hidden in model number suffixes. USB interface speed limit? Buried in the spec table. Actual $/TB? You have to calculate it yourself — and the capacity listed in the title is often wrong.

This tracker does the work for you: parses capacity from titles, calculates $/TB, labels every drive's technology and interface, and flags warranty and condition on every listing.

🗄️ For Hard Drives: CMR vs SMR

CMR (Conventional Magnetic Recording) = RAID-safe, NAS-safe, sustained write performance. SMR (Shingled Magnetic Recording) = cheaper, but causes RAID rebuild failures and NAS warnings. This tracker labels every drive.

⚡ For External SSDs: MB/s vs Interface

Advertised "1000 MB/s read" is meaningless if your port only supports 500 MB/s. This tracker shows both the drive's rated speed and the interface's theoretical max — so you know what you'll actually get.
